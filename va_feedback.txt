## Formal, non-attacking comments you can give

### 1. On GPT-4o being central

> One suggestion is to use GPT-4o more as a secondary reasoning or summarization layer rather than the primary adjudication engine. For this use case, the most business-critical outputs are the exact outcome class, trigger, and reviewer-facing evidence. Those may be safer if they come first from structured multimodal features and policy logic, with GPT-4o helping to generate a polished narrative or causal summary afterward.

### Real-world example

> For example, in a hard-brake event with a pedestrian entering the lane, we need the final “Near Collision” vs “Possible Collision” decision to be fully traceable to braking telemetry, actor trajectory, and policy rules, rather than mainly to an LLM interpretation.

---

### 2. On telemetry needing to be first-class

> Since telemetry is available, I think we can strengthen the architecture by elevating it to a top-level branch in the main decision flow. For driving safety events, telemetry is not just supporting context; in many cases it is primary evidence for braking, acceleration, harsh maneuvers, and event severity.

### Real-world example

> A strong brake event over a speed bump and a strong brake event to avoid a pedestrian can look visually similar in a short clip, but telemetry plus scene reasoning helps separate “rough surface” from a true near-collision scenario.

---

### 3. On scope control for Phase 1

> Another suggestion is to separate clearly between Phase-1 required outputs and broader reason or behavior coverage. The current taxonomy and behavior catalog are valuable, but it may be useful to distinguish between what must be delivered reliably in Phase 1 and what can be introduced as high-confidence supporting tags later.

### Real-world example

> In the first release, it may be enough to guarantee accurate outcome, trigger, evidence summary, and maybe one to three high-confidence behavior reasons. Broader behavior coverage such as awareness, conduct, or condition can then be layered in once the core review path is stable.

---

### 4. On the video stack

> I think the current video path is good for a prototype, but for a production-oriented collision and near-collision review system, it may benefit from a stronger structured scene-understanding layer, especially around lane/drivable context, range ordering, relative motion, and conflict metrics.

### Real-world example

> For instance, distinguishing “critical distance” from “lane departure” or identifying whether a lead vehicle was actually in-path often depends on drivable-area reasoning, relative motion, and TTC-like proxies, not only object detection and tracking.

---

### 5. On fixed 0.5-second windows

> The 0.5-second windowing is a good simplification for initial aggregation, but I would suggest not making it the only temporal representation. Some safety-critical cues happen at a finer resolution than a fixed half-second bucket.

### Real-world example

> Brake onset, hazard first visibility, minimum TTC, and an impact-like audio spike may occur within a narrower time band. If we only use 0.5-second windows, we may lose some fidelity in event sequencing and causal attribution.

---

### 6. On the 250+ rule table

> The rule table is useful as a requirements and coverage artifact, but I would recommend using rules primarily for gating, escalation, contradiction checks, and policy compliance, rather than as the main bridge from raw detections to final classification.

### Real-world example

> In real driving data, rule interactions can become brittle quickly. A “vehicle_close + brake_squeal” rule may work well in many cases, but edge cases like road noise, camera shake, or lane confusion can produce false matches unless we also use structured learned reasoning and confidence propagation.

---

### 7. On SME validation

> I think the SME knowledge-base layer is very important. One refinement could be to make deterministic KB logic the primary compliance mechanism, and use retrieval or RAG more for support, examples, and analyst assistance rather than as the first validation mechanism.

### Real-world example

> For an exact policy boundary like “Near Collision - Unavoidable” versus “Near Collision,” deterministic rule interpretation against structured event states may be more consistent than semantic retrieval alone.

My suggestion would be to preserve that direction, but make the Phase-1 production path more structured: keep telemetry as a first-class modality, use stronger scene/kinematic reasoning before final adjudication, keep LLMs in a secondary reasoning or summarization role, and use SME policy logic deterministically for compliance-critical decisions. That would help us keep the speed of the current concept while improving traceability, robustness, and reviewer trust.
